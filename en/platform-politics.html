Manifesto for Planetary Governance

Politics is not a struggle for power. It is the collective responsibility for the health of our shared home. We reject the outdated model of geopolitics where fortress-nations compete for resources. We propose a new paradigm: geo-governance—a collaborative, science-based, and ethically-aligned process of caring for the "Humanity + Biosphere" system.

Key Principles:

  • Goal of Politics is Health, Not Growth: The success of a government is measured by the cleanliness of its rivers, the level of social trust, and the well-being of future generations, not by figures of production and consumption.
  • The Politician as a System Engineer: Their task is not to defend group interests but to find optimal solutions for the entire ecosystem, based on data and long-term forecasting.
  • Sovereignty as Responsibility, Not Permission: The right to pollute one's territory or develop dangerous technologies cannot be a sovereign right, as it threatens the entire planetary organism.
