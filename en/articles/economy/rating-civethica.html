The Civethica Rating: A New Metric for a World Tired of Deception

We're used to measuring corporate success in financial terms. It's time to measure it by its contribution to the health of our civilization.

We live in an age of paradoxes. A company can claim to champion "sustainable development" while destroying ecosystems thousands of miles from its headquarters. A brand can speak of "social responsibility" while using slave labor in the developing world. We see dazzling financial reports and hear bold pronouncements, yet we intuitively feel that something is hidden behind the facade.

The existing system for evaluating business is fundamentally broken. It is built on the illusion that financial profit is synonymous with success. This illusion allows "greenwashing" and "ethics-washing" to thrive, where attractive marketing conceals a toxic reality.

The Civethica Foundation proposes a radically new approach: The Civethica Rating for Business. This is not just another set of criteria. It is a new coordinate system designed to answer a single question: What is the true systemic contribution of this company to the health of the planet, society, and human consciousness?

Why Old Metrics Fail

Imagine two companies.

  • Company A produces ultra-cheap "fast fashion." Its financial performance is spectacular, with staggering profits and a soaring stock price. But what is its true cost? The depletion of water resources, microplastic pollution in our oceans, a culture of overconsumption, and the exploitation of low-wage labor. Systemically, this company takes more from the world than it gives.
  • Company B develops water purification technology and implements it in developing nations. Its profits may be modest. But its systemic contribution is immense: it improves the health of millions, helps restore ecosystems, and promotes social stability. Systemically, this company heals the world.

In the current financial paradigm, Company A is a "success," while Company B is a "niche project." The Civethica Rating is designed to turn this logic on its head.

The Three Dimensions of True Contribution

We evaluate companies along three interconnected axes that cover their entire lifecycle—from concept to impact.

1. The Value Creation Cycle: "What You Do and How You Do It"

This is an analysis of the production chain and the product itself.

  • Product Ethics and Impact on Human Health: This is a direct and uncompromising criterion. We analyze whether a company's product makes people's lives better or worse.
    • Positive Contribution: Developing life-saving drugs, producing healthy food, creating educational technologies, or tools for mental well-being.
    • Negative Contribution: Manufacturing products that cause addiction (tobacco, alcohol, gambling), contribute to chronic diseases (ultra-processed foods high in sugar and fat), or create a harmful information environment.
  • Ecological Ethics: How do you use resources? How do you minimize waste? Do you restore nature or deplete it?
  • Social Ethics: What are the working conditions for your employees and contractors? Is the pay fair? Are human rights respected at all stages of production?

2. The Value Distribution Cycle: "Where Do Your Money and Influence Go?"

This is our key differentiator from other ratings. We believe it is not enough to produce a "clean" product. It is critically important what the resulting profit and influence are spent on.

A company can produce the world's most organic yogurt, but if its profits are used to finance a political party that incites war, to support terrorist organizations, or to organize disinformation campaigns, its systemic contribution is deeply negative.

Here, we assess:

  • Political Influence: Who does the company fund and lobby for? Does it promote peace and democracy or militarism and authoritarianism?
  • Informational Influence: Does the company fight disinformation or is it a source of it? Does it engage in "informational terror" against competitors or entire communities?
  • Tax Ethics: Does the company pay taxes where it earns profits, or does it move money to offshore havens, depriving society of funds for healthcare and education?
  • Ownership Structure: Who are the ultimate beneficiaries of the company? Are there criminal or sanctioned entities behind it?

3. The Principle of Openness: "Can You Be Trusted?"

Trust is impossible without transparency. Therefore, the methodology of the Civethica Rating will be entirely open-source. Any expert, journalist, or citizen will be able to review our data and understand how the assessment was formed. We are not issuing a verdict—we are providing the tools for an informed conclusion.

A Future We Can Choose

The Civethica Rating is not just an evaluation tool. It is a lever for transformation. It is needed by:

  • Conscious investors to direct capital toward companies that are building a healthy future.
  • Thoughtful consumers to make their purchases a vote in support of real values.
  • Talented employees to choose employers whose mission aligns with their own.
  • Companies themselves to get an honest map of their systemic impact and find pathways for genuine improvement.

We invite experts, analysts, researchers, and all concerned citizens to join the dialogue in creating and developing this vital tool. It is time to demand not just financial, but civilizational responsibility from business.
