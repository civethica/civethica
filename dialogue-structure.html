Structure of The Planetary Dialogue

The Planetary Dialogue is not a single event, but a continuous, multi-level process designed to ensure inclusivity, expertise, and practical outcomes. Its structure is based on the principles of transparency, decentralization, and evidence-based decision-making.

Phase 1: The Thematic Cycles

The Dialogue operates in thematic cycles, each lasting 6-12 months and dedicated to a specific global challenge (e.g., "Climate and Biosphere," "Global Health," "Digital Ethics"). Each cycle consists of three interconnected levels:

  • Global Expert Panels: Small, dedicated groups of leading scientists, philosophers, and practitioners who prepare foundational reports, define key questions, and analyze data.
  • Regional Online Forums: Moderated discussions open to the public in all major languages. Here, the expert findings are debated, and regional perspectives and local knowledge are collected.
  • Community Dialogues: We provide tools and methodologies for local groups (universities, NGOs, community centers) to host their own discussions, the results of which are fed back into the regional forums.

Phase 2: The Synthesis Assembly

At the end of each cycle, elected representatives from the regional forums, along with expert panel members, convene in a Synthesis Assembly. Their task is to integrate the vast amount of information gathered and formulate concrete proposals and ethical guidelines based on the discussions. This process ensures that final recommendations are both scientifically sound and socially legitimized.

Phase 3: The "Future Charter"

The proposals from all thematic cycles will be gradually integrated into a dynamic, evolving document: the "Future Charter". This document is intended to serve as a moral and ethical reference point for policymakers, corporations, and individuals worldwide. It is not a binding law, but a compass for building a sustainable and humane civilization.
